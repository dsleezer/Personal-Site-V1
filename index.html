About Me

My name is David Sleezer and I am an aspiring full stack web developer. In my early 20's I had no idea what I wanted to do so I went out and got a job at a retail store. Shortly after starting that I decided that whatever I do, I need to make a career out of it so I focused on learning the business and trying to advance. After many long days, and weeks that ran together my hard work paid off and during my 6th year in retail became an Assistant Store Manager.

A few years later I met my wife and decided that I could no longer balance consistently working 60-80 hour work weeks and having a healthy home life so I began to look at more consistent work environments. Through friends I found a job in Insurance Underwriting but it was a reset on my career. I entered Underwriting with a renewed vigor knowing that I had to learn everything I could and push myself in order to advance again. I got my first promotion 6 months after starting which was the minimum time required. I continued to learn the new role and technical knowledge in order to continue advancing and eventually was promoted to a Senior Underwriter.

Shortly after this promotion I found out that the entire underwriting department was going to get laid off in approximately 1 year as the company had failed to retain the contract that I was working on. With this much notice I took my time to decide what I wanted to go going forward. Growing up I was not the best student in school, I had attempted to go to college right out of high school but didn't know what I wanted to do so didn't apply myself very well. Swapping from the retail field into a desk job originally terrified me because I had spent so long always moving around and only sitting down at my desk when I had to plan something or place merchandise orders and as soon as I could I would be back on my feet. I learned quickly as an underwriter that I could focus on a task and be engaged whether it was learning the requirements for different types of transactions, processing high volumes of transactions, or helping customers through the documentation that needs to be submitted and I didn't need to be moving around constantly to be engaged with what I was doing.

I have always enjoyed working with computers, from playing games with friends, building my own computers, setting up servers for me and my friends to play on, to figuring out how to apply modifications, but I had never learned how to write code on my own. I took this opportunity provided by being able to prepare for almost a year and set myself up to focus on a course designed to teach a working knowledge of Full Stack Web Development. I plan to use this to begin a new career utilizing the ability to manage tasks that I learned in retail, the focus on details while not losing sight of the big picture that I learned in underwriting, and the technical skills that I am currently learning at Centriq.
